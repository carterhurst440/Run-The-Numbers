# How to Play

Rules pulled directly from the in-game **How to Play** modals.

---

## Run the Numbers

### Core Loop
1. Place bets on any combination of wager regions before the hand.
2. Cards are dealt one at a time from a shuffled 53-card deck (a standard 52 plus one Joker).
3. The hand continues until a Stopper card appears — ending the hand.
4. All open bets resolve based on the cards dealt and the stopper.

### Wager Regions
- Bet on card counts, suit runs, color streaks, rank thresholds, and more.
- Each region has its own paytable and stopper rules.
- Multiple bets can be active in the same hand.

### Stopper Cards
Stoppers are always the face cards plus the Joker — every J, Q, and K (12 cards) and the single Joker, for 13 stoppers in every deck. The moment any of them is dealt, the hand ends and all bets resolve against the final card count and hand state.

### Auto Deal & Auto Rebet
Enable Auto Deal to draw cards automatically without clicking after each card. Enable Auto Rebet to carry your previous wagers into the next hand automatically.

## Color Scheme

### The Round
Each round has **3 rolls**. Every roll lands a color die (R·Y·B) and a number die (1–6). The number adds to that color's running total.

### Scoring
- **Primary score** = Red + Yellow + Blue totals combined.
- **Secondary score** = Orange + Green + Purple totals combined.
- **Grand total** = Primary + Secondary.

### Color Totals
Numbers don't always just add — adjacency between rolls changes how totals are formed.

- **Secondaries multiply** — When two consecutive rolls land on touching primary colors (R+Y → Orange, Y+B → Green, R+B → Purple), those two numbers **multiply** to form the secondary total rather than adding to the primaries. Example: Roll 1 lands Red 4, Roll 2 lands Yellow 3 → Orange gets 12, not Red +4 and Yellow +3.
- **Same color in a row multiplies** — Two consecutive rolls on the same primary color **multiply** instead of add. Roll 1 Red 5, Roll 2 Red 3 → Red gets 15, not 8.
- **Three in a row chains** — All three rolls on the same color multiply in sequence — each number by the next. Red 2 · Red 3 · Red 4 → 2 × 3 × 4 = 24.
- **Only adjacent rolls combine** — Only Roll 1+2 or Roll 2+3 can form a secondary. Roll 1 and Roll 3 never touch — even if they land on combining colors, they will not form a secondary together. A maximum of two secondaries can form in a single round.

### Tips
Place all bets before the first roll — the mat locks when rolling begins. Primary and Secondary bets pay on the combined team score. Color bets pay on which single color accumulated the most points across all 3 rolls.
